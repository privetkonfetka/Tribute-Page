Vladimir Nabokov

Great Russian writer

Here's a time line of Vladimir Nabokov's life:

  • April 22, 1899: Vladimir Nabokov is born in St. Petersburg, Russia, into an aristocratic family.
  • 1919: Nabokov and his family flee Russia following the Russian Revolution and settle in Crimea.
  • 1922-1925: Nabokov studies at Trinity College, Cambridge, where he completes his undergraduate studies.
  • 1925: Nabokov's family moves to Berlin, where he begins his career as a writer and publishes his first poems and novels in Russian.
  • 1937: Nabokov marries Véra Slonim, and the couple eventually has one son, Dmitri.
  • 1940: The Nabokovs emigrate to the United States to escape the growing threat of World War II in Europe.
  • 1941-1948: Nabokov teaches literature at several American universities, including Wellesley College and Cornell University.
  • 1955: Nabokov publishes "Lolita," a controversial and highly acclaimed novel that becomes his most famous work.
  • 1961: Nabokov becomes a naturalized American citizen.
  • 1969: Nabokov publishes "Ada or Ardor: A Family Chronicle."
  • 1972: Nabokov's wife, Véra, dies.
  • 1975: Nabokov dies on July 2 in Montreux, Switzerland.
